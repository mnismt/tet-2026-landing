What Tết means

Tết is Vietnam’s most important holiday—welcoming a new lunar year with gratitude, hope, and wishes for prosperity.

How it feels

Streets brighten with flowers and lights, homes fill with shared meals, and every greeting carries a fresh-start spirit.

Brief guide to Tết traditions

Tết traditions blend family values, spiritual gratitude, and symbolic acts meant to invite good fortune for the year ahead.

Fresh start

Homes are cleaned and decorated so the new year begins with clarity, warmth, and welcoming energy.

Family reunion

Meals bring generations together, making time for stories, gratitude, and wishes for health and success.

Respect & remembrance

Many families honor ancestors with offerings and prayers—grounding the celebration in heritage and care.

Festive foods

Two iconic rice cakes often shared during Tết—comforting, symbolic, and made for togetherness.

Bánh chưng

Traditional

A square sticky-rice cake filled with mung bean and pork, wrapped in leaves and slow-cooked—often symbolizing the earth and family unity.

Bánh tét

Regional favorite

A cylindrical sticky-rice cake with savory or sweet fillings, sliced into rounds—popular in the south and perfect for sharing.

Activities & customs

Small gestures carry big meaning—these customs help welcome luck, harmony, and joy.

Lì xì

Red envelopes are given as blessings—often to children and elders—for luck, health, and prosperity.

Visiting family

Families visit relatives and neighbors, exchanging New Year wishes and strengthening bonds.

Ancestor altar

Offerings and incense express gratitude and invite ancestral blessings for the household.

First-footing

The first visitor of the new year is believed to influence the household’s luck—often chosen with care.

Fireworks

Light and sound welcome the year with excitement—where permitted, they heighten the festive atmosphere.

Flowers & décor

Peach blossoms, apricot blossoms, kumquat trees, and lantern tones add color and symbolic prosperity.

A gentle checklist for a calm, meaningful celebration—perfect for mobile-friendly browsing.
